üí°Solved: Fixing Lenovo ThinkPad Touchscreen Issues (The Registry Fix)

Date: 2025-11-19

If you're supporting Lenovo ThinkPad Gen4 (or similar) laptops and suddenly the touchscreen stops responding for a specific user, you're not alone. The usual suspects‚Äîdrivers and BIOS settings‚Äîoften don't fix it. Through a structured troubleshooting process, I discovered this is often caused by corrupt or misconfigured user-specific touch and pen calibration settings stored in the Windows Registry. Here is the step-by-step fix that resolved the issue.

‚ö†Ô∏èImportant Warning -You are editing the Windows Registry. Always back up the registry first (File > Export) before proceeding.

‚öôÔ∏èThe Simple Steps:

Step 1: Open the Registry Editor Press the Windows Key + R, type regedit, and hit Enter.

Step 2: Reset Touch and Pen Calibration

-Navigate to: HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\

-Find the folder (key) named Wisp.

-Right-click the Wisp key and choose Rename.

-Rename it to Wisp.old. (Windows will create a new, clean one automatically).

Step 3: Clean Up Optional Settings

While still in the HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\ path, look for the following two keys:

-TouchPrediction (If you see it, right-click and Delete it).

-TabletTip (If you see it, right-click and Rename it to TabletTip.old).

Step 4: Restart

Restart the computer. When the affected user logs back in, the system will generate fresh, non-corrupt touch settings, and your touchscreen should be working again!
